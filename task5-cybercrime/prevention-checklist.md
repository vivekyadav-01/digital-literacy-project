# Prevention Checklist

-  Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or bank account password with anyone, not even someone claiming to be from your bank or payment app. Banks never ask for these details.
-  Always find customer care numbers from the official app settings or the bank's official website, never from a general Google search.
-  Never install remote access applications like AnyDesk, TeamViewer, or QuickSupport on someone else's instruction. This is one of the most common UPI fraud techniques.
-  Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on all your accounts including email, social media, and payment apps wherever available.
-  Use unique, strong passwords for each platform. Avoid using your name, date of birth, or common words. Use a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols.
-  Check the URL before entering login credentials. Fraudulent websites often use URLs that look similar to real ones, for example `paytm-help.com` instead of `paytm.com`.
-  Do not click on links received via SMS, WhatsApp, or email from unknown senders, even if they appear to be from a bank or government portal.
-  Regularly check your bank statements and UPI transaction history. Report any unrecognised transaction immediately to your bank and on `cybercrime.gov.in`.
-  If you receive a collect request on your UPI app from an unknown party, do not approve it. You should only enter your PIN to send money, never to receive it.
-  In case of fraud, call the National Cyber Crime Helpline `1930` immediately. File a formal report at `cybercrime.gov.in` as soon as possible.
