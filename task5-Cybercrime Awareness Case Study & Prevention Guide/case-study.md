# Case Study: The Fake Customer Care Trap

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the fastest-growing forms of cybercrime in India, particularly targeting college students, first-time smartphone users, and rural populations. The following is a realistic scenario based on commonly reported fraud patterns.

Riya, a 19-year-old first-year student at a central university, had recently linked her bank account to a popular UPI app. One evening, her transaction to pay a mess bill failed, and the amount was debited from her account. She searched online for the UPI app's customer care number and found a result that appeared legitimate. She called the number, and a person claiming to be a support agent asked her to download a remote access application to "resolve the issue".

Once the application was installed, the fraudster gained control of Riya's screen and navigated to her UPI app. He then asked her to "verify" her account by entering her UPI PIN. Within seconds, three transactions totalling Rs. 22,000 were made from her account to unknown beneficiaries. The call disconnected immediately after.

## Fraud Pattern

The crime followed a clear pattern:

1. The victim encounters a transaction issue.
2. The fraudster plants a fake support number in search results.
3. The victim calls and is asked to install a remote access tool.
4. The victim is psychologically manipulated into revealing their PIN.
5. Funds are drained before the victim understands what happened.

## Why Students Are Targeted

College students are a primary target because many are using UPI apps independently for the first time and may not be aware that no legitimate bank or payment app will ever ask for your PIN, OTP, or remote access permissions.

## Consequences

The consequences of such fraud can include:

- Financial loss
- Emotional distress
- The time-intensive process of filing a cybercrime complaint

## Key Lesson

Students must verify customer care contacts only through official app or bank websites and should never share their UPI PIN, OTP, or grant remote access to anyone claiming to provide support.
